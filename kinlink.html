The Problem

Aging parents left behind as care moves online.

Healthcare, insurance, and billing have moved into portals and apps. Many older adults can't keep up with passwords, paperwork, and unfamiliar interfaces.

Adult children try to help from a distance — through scattered texts, calls, and shared notes. Reminders get lost. Siblings duplicate work. Cross-border families feel it most.

The cost: missed checkups, medication errors, and constant stress on both sides.

01

Scattered coordination

Texts, calls, screenshots, and spreadsheets — nothing in one place, nothing in sync.

02

Tools built for the patient

Most caregiver apps assume the elder is the primary user. The adult child is left to improvise.

03

Time zones & distance

Families split between continents juggle midnight reminders and two healthcare systems.

04

Sibling friction

Without a shared view of "who's doing what," tasks fall through cracks — or get done twice.

Our MVP

One shared dashboard for the whole family.

A mobile app for adult children to coordinate parents' health and life tasks — from anywhere in the world.

Accessibility
How it works

Set up your care circle in minutes — start coordinating in one.

i

Invite your family

Add your parents, siblings, and anyone else who helps. Each person picks their role and what they want to see.

ii

Add what's on your plate

Appointments, prescriptions, bills, documents. KinLink turns them into shared, time-zone-aware tasks.

iii

Coordinate without chasing

Reminders, proxy actions, and a shared timeline keep everyone in sync — without group-chat overload.

Unique Value

The remote control center for family caregiving — without forcing elders to master complex health tech.

i.

Caregiver-first

Built for the adult child, not the patient. Every workflow assumes you're acting on a parent's behalf.

ii.

Cross-border ready

Time-zone aware reminders, multi-language profiles, and proxy actions made for families split across continents.

iii.

Elder-friendly

Parents see only what they need. The complexity stays on the caregiver's side — never forced onto the elder.
